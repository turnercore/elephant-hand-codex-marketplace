# Videos and third-party resources

## Curate reality before generating it

For physical or visually intricate skills, use web and image search when available to find real demonstrations. Inspect the source page and the media itself. A model-generated diagram must not be the sole authority for a knot, repair, craft, body movement, instrument technique, laboratory procedure, or other sequence where spatial accuracy matters.

Prefer a short expert video, controllable animation, or sourced image sequence. Use generated media only to simplify one supporting concept that you can independently verify.

## Purposeful segments

Do not paste a video into a lesson without framing it. Provide:

- creator and title
- relevant start and optional end time
- expected viewing duration
- why this segment is useful
- a prediction or observation prompt before viewing
- a task after viewing
- a transcript, summary, article, diagram, or direct explanation alternative

The rest of the lesson must remain coherent if the embed is offline, blocked, removed, or inaccessible.

## Guided external resources

Use `GuidedResource` for step-through animations, interactive diagrams, reference pages, and demonstrations that should remain on their original site. State what to inspect, what to predict beforehand, and what the learner should reconstruct or perform afterward. Link to the original rather than copying protected media.

## Embedding

Prefer privacy-enhanced YouTube embeds when available. Do not autoplay. Use a stable aspect ratio and include a normal external link. Mark external content clearly. Respect the source site's embedding policy.

## Quotes

Quote only the smallest useful excerpt. Preserve attribution and context. Paraphrase when the precise wording is not itself the object of study. Do not copy substantial protected text, transcripts, articles, or book sections into the course.

## Images and diagrams

Use original, public-domain, openly licensed, or permission-compatible assets. Record creator, source URL, license, and modifications. Always provide alt text. When a remote asset is essential, consider storing a permitted local copy and recording provenance. A search-result thumbnail is not permission or provenance.
