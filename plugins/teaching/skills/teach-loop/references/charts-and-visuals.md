# Charts and visual explanations

## Choose by task

- compare quantities: bar or dot plot
- trend over ordered time: line chart
- relationship between variables: scatterplot
- distribution: histogram, density, or interval plot
- part-to-whole: use sparingly; often a bar is clearer
- sequence or causality: annotated timeline or flow diagram
- spatial relationship: SVG diagram or 3D scene
- parameter effect: interactive chart with controlled sliders

## Lesson chart contract

A chart needs:

- the question it helps answer
- clear units and scale
- truthful axes and encodings
- alt text or textual summary
- a prompt before or during inspection
- a follow-up interpretation or transfer task
- source information for data

Do not use color as the only distinction. Avoid chart junk, unlabeled axes, and
interaction that hides the key comparison.
