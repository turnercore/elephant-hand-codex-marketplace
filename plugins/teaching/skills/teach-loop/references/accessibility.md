# Accessibility

## Baseline

- semantic headings and landmarks
- visible keyboard focus
- keyboard-operable controls
- sufficient contrast in light and dark modes
- labels and descriptions for form controls
- no information conveyed by color alone
- responsive layout without horizontal traps
- reduced-motion behavior
- captions, transcript, or alternative for video
- alt text and source for images
- textual summary for charts and 3D scenes
- clear feedback announced with appropriate live regions

## Interactions

Dragging must have a click, keyboard, select, or numeric alternative. Hover-only
content must also appear on focus or activation. Timed work must be optional or
adjustable unless timing is the skill being practiced.

## Cognitive accessibility

Use plain instructions, one primary task at a time, predictable component
behavior, and progressive disclosure. Avoid dense decorative UI around the
learning task.
