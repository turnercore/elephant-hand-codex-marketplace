<Callout title="This is not a placement exam" kind="insight">
  The goal is to give your tutor a useful first map. Uncertainty is excellent information. Use the note buttons whenever a thought appears that does not fit the prompt in front of you.
</Callout>

<LessonSection id="mission" title="Name the change you want" eyebrow="Mission">
  A strong mission points to something you want to make, decide, perceive, explain, or do. “Understand the topic” is usually too foggy to steer a tutor.

  <PredictionPrompt
    id="mission-outcome-prediction"
    prompt="Imagine this learning worked unusually well. What would you be able to do, make, or understand that you cannot reliably do now?"
    placeholder="A concrete outcome, project, decision, performance, or change…"
    confidence={false}
  />
</LessonSection>

<LessonSection id="starting-point" title="Show the tutor your current model" eyebrow="Light diagnosis">
  Choose the description closest to your current relationship with the subject. This does not lock you into a level.

  <MultipleChoice
    id="current-relationship"
    prompt="Where are you starting?"
    options={[
      { id: "new", label: "Mostly new: I need a useful first mental model." },
      { id: "fragmented", label: "I know fragments, but they do not connect yet." },
      { id: "procedural", label: "I can follow examples, but I struggle to work independently." },
      { id: "transfer", label: "I know the basics and want to apply them flexibly in real work." },
    ]}
  />

  <Checkpoint
    id="current-model"
    prompt="Without looking anything up, explain what you currently think the subject is really about. Include anything you suspect you may be misunderstanding."
    hints={[
      "Describe one important object, process, or relationship in the subject.",
      "Name a term you have heard but do not yet trust yourself to use.",
      "Say what tends to happen when you try to apply the subject in practice.",
    ]}
  />
</LessonSection>

<LessonSection id="constraints" title="Design for the real learner" eyebrow="Fit">
  Your tutor can adapt pacing, examples, visual density, practice style, and the shape of the next lesson. A preference is a starting hypothesis, not a permanent <ConceptTooltip term="learning-style label">a fixed label that makes a flexible preference look like a permanent identity</ConceptTooltip>.

  <Checkpoint
    id="constraints-and-preferences"
    prompt="What constraints, tools, examples, access needs, or teaching approaches should the tutor know about? What has helped or annoyed you in the past?"
    hints={[
      "Consider available time, deadlines, software, equipment, or budget.",
      "Mention whether examples from your own projects would help.",
      "Include readability, motion, audio, caption, input, or attention needs when relevant.",
    ]}
  />
</LessonSection>

<LessonSection id="first-question" title="Give the tutor a thread to pull" eyebrow="Next action">
  <ReflectionPrompt
    id="first-open-question"
    prompt="What is the first question, confusion, or small capability you would most like the tutor to investigate with you?"
  />
</LessonSection>
